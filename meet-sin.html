Meet Sin

Sin the Kin works through catharsis, negative space, and capricious emotion.

Creating art is a cathartic release for me.

I learned how to draw when I was 16. The COVID pandemic began in my junior year of high school, where I experienced an intense period of isolation. My view of the world was articulated through technology, where I absorbed an abundant amount of human evil, from hate crimes to crimes against humanity.

From this, however, I also perceived the wonderful world of creativity. I was given powerful emotions and inspirations which birthed my ability to produce art. This is why my work captures the definition of capricious.

I specialize in black and white work because I am fascinated with using negative space. This method allows me to dictate the viewer's attention. I create both digitally and traditionally. My digital work is made in Clip Studio Paint, where I can also animate, and my traditional work is made with acrylic paint.

My style is heavily influenced by Leonardo Da Vinci's sketches, namely hatching and blending. While my style is consistent, the meaning of each piece differs, from lighthearted to grave.
